What is
Diabetic Retinopathy ?

Diabetic retinopathy is an eye condition that occurs when diabetes damages the blood vessels and nerve tissue in the retina.
Explanation :
High blood sugar levels from diabetes can damage the retina, the light-sensitive tissue in the back of the eye. This can lead to vision loss or blindness if left untreated.
Stages :
Diabetic retinopathy has two stages:
Nonproliferative retinopathy : The early stage, where blood vessels swell and leak. This can cause mild vision loss and macular edema, which is swelling of the retina.
Proliferative retinopathy : The advanced stage, where abnormal new blood vessels grow on the retina's surface. These vessels can break and bleed into the vitreous, causing severe vision loss.
Risk factors : The longer you have diabetes and the less controlled your blood sugar is, the more likely you are to develop diabetic retinopathy.
