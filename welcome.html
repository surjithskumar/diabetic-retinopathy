How is it treated ?

Treatment for diabetic retinopathy depends on the severity of the condition, your age, general health, and symptoms. Treatments include :
Laser Trearment
Used to shrink abnormal blood vessels or seal leaking ones. This can help reduce swelling in the retina.
Eye injections
Injections of anti-VEGF drugs or corticosteroids can slow or reverse diabetic retinopathy.
Eye surgery
A vitrectomy is a procedure that removes the vitreous, the jelly-like substance that fills the center of the eye. This surgery may be needed if there is a lot of blood or scar tissue in the eye.
Steroid eye implants
These implants can be placed in the eye to release a small amount of corticosteroid over time.
Lifestyle modifications
These may include insulin and other medications, dietary changes, and exercise.
Blood pressure control
Tightly controlling blood pressure can help prevent and manage diabetic retinopathy.

Diabetic retinopathy doesn't have a cure, but treatment can help prevent, delay, or reduce vision loss. People with type 1 diabetes should have a complete eye exam within five years of diagnosis, and those with type 2 should have an eye exam at the time of diagnosis. After that, people with diabetes should have an eye exam at least once a year
